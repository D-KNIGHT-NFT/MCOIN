Limbus (Latin for "edge", "hem", "boundary"), more precisely Limbus corneae, describes the transition zone between the horn (cornea) and sclera of the eyeball.
In Catholic theology refers to a place ( also popularly known as "antechamber" or "outermost circle of hell") where souls are, who are or were excluded from heaven and the eternal vision of God through no fault of their own, which was traditionally considered to be the place for the souls of children who died unbaptized.
